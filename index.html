Vote centers allow any registered voter within a jurisdiction to cast a ballot at any polling location on Election Day, rather than being assigned to a specific precinct. This model requires real-time voter check-in systems and has seen rapid adoption across the Midwest and Plains regions in particular.

Research Findings

Key Results

Administrative Efficiency

Vote centers dramatically reduce the operational footprint of election administration. Jurisdictions report roughly half as many physical polling locations to staff and maintain, and corresponding reductions in poll worker recruitment needs.

Provisional Ballot Reduction

Because vote centers eliminate "wrong precinct" errors, jurisdictions see sharp reductions in both the number of provisional ballots cast and the share of provisionals that are ultimately rejected.

Voter Location Experience

Survey data from 2024 show that vote center users reported somewhat less ease locating their polling place compared to precinct voters — an important equity and communication consideration for implementing jurisdictions.

Implementation Quality Matters

The research finds that vote centers prove most effective in larger jurisdictions and when paired with robust voter communication. Access and equity require ongoing attention, particularly for voters with limited transportation or information access.
